ICP Framework
Who we target
in Canada —
and why.
An ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) is a precise description of the customer most likely to buy, derive value from, and stay. Not everyone who could benefit — the specific profile where pain is highest, the purchase decision is fastest, and the product delivers the most obvious value.
The two profiles
ICP 1
The Stretched Solo
A solo independent advisor carrying a heavy client book with no support staff, doing all admin personally. The pain is entirely their own. The decision is entirely theirs.
Fast close 14-day cycle Single buyer
ICP 2
The Growing Team
A named advisory practice of 2–5 advisors that has recently grown, with inconsistent documentation across the team. Two buyers — the managing partner and the associates — who care about different things.
Higher ACV 21–30 day cycle Multi-buyer

Why they buy
They are their own bottleneck. Every hour spent on admin is an hour not spent with a client or acquiring new ones. The pain is personal, immediate, and visible in their own calendar every week. There is no one else to blame and no one else to convince.
How we find them
1
Public directories
IPC and Assante both publish public advisor locators on their websites. Filter by province — Ontario and BC first for highest density. Pull every solo advisor name and cross-reference on LinkedIn.
Why: Zero cost, immediately available, 700+ IPC and 900+ Assante advisors.
2
LinkedIn signal filter
Search LinkedIn by firm name. Filter for advisors who have posted in the last 30 days — these are active, visible, and will respond. Prioritise anyone who has posted about client service, time, or workflow.
Why: Active LinkedIn users reply to outreach at 2–3× the rate of dormant profiles.
3
Champion referrals
Jason Pereira (IPC) and Christian Battistelli (Assante) are named Focal users. A direct introduction from either of them reaches their peer network instantly — with trust pre-loaded.
Why: Referrals account for 67% of new client acquisition in the advisory industry. (Schwab RIA Benchmarking, 2024)
4
Industry events
Advocis national events, IPC and Assante annual advisor conferences, FP Canada's annual congress. High-quality conversations at lower volume. One live demo in a room of five advisors outperforms 50 cold emails.
Why: Event-sourced leads convert at 40% opportunity-to-close — the highest of any channel. (B2B Funnel Benchmarks, 2025)

ICP 2 — Acquisition Framework
The Growing
Advisory Team
A named advisory practice of 2–5 advisors that has recently added a team member, creating workflow inconsistency. Two distinct buyers in the same room: the managing partner who approves the spend, and the associates who use it daily.
Profile at a glance
Target network
IPC · Assante · Wellington-Altus

Target cycle

Per seat

The two buyers — different pain, different pitch
Managing partner
Economic buyer
Cares about compliance consistency across the team, audit risk, oversight, and operational efficiency at a firm level. Approves the spend. Needs to be able to defend the decision to a regulator.
Associate advisor
End user + champion
Cares about their own daily time savings — notes, follow-ups, CRM updates. If they love it, they become the internal advocate who closes the managing partner from the inside.
How we find them
1
Named practice filter
In network directories, filter for practices with a branded firm name rather than just "John Smith, IPC" — that signals a team. Cross-reference on LinkedIn for team size and recent hires.
Why: A named practice signals enough scale for inconsistency to be a real problem, not a theoretical one.
2
Recent growth signal
LinkedIn shows when someone adds "Associate at [Practice Name]" to their profile. A new team addition in the last 18 months means workflow inconsistency is fresh and unsolved.
Why: Growth creates the exact problem Focal solves. New hire = new documentation habits entering an existing workflow.
3
Industry award lists
Wealth Professional 5-Star Advisory Teams, Investment Executive Report Cards, Advisors' Report Card — all published annually with named practices. Teams that win are investing in excellence and receptive to tools.
Why: Award-winning teams have demonstrated commitment to raising their standard. They are the most receptive buyer profile.
4
B2 close → enterprise bridge
Every closed B2 deal creates an expansion opportunity. Ask the managing partner to share their experience with the network's practice management team. One reference to a network head is worth 200 cold outreach messages.
Why: This is the bridge from Bucket 2 to an enterprise motion. Build it into every close from day one.

Team-specific objections
"Our advisors all have different workflows — one tool won't work for everyone.
That inconsistency is exactly the problem Focal solves, not a reason to avoid it. Focal standardises the output — the note format, the CRM sync, the follow-up structure — while each advisor still runs their meeting their own way. The client sees consistency. The advisor keeps autonomy.
"We'd need our whole team to adopt it — that's a change management challenge.
That's why we start with your most tech-forward advisor on a 14-day trial first. When they show the rest of the team what their meetings look like now versus before, the adoption happens from the inside. You don't manage the change — they do it for you.
